## 2. What never enters the workspace

Credentials and passwords · payroll and HR records · customer identity documents · anything under legal privilege · anything you would not hand a new contractor on day one.

## 3. Autonomy levels

| May run without asking | Must always ask first |
|---|---|
| Read-and-report (briefs, summaries, analyses) | Anything that **sends** (email, messages, invoices) |
| Drafts saved to the workspace for review | Anything that **spends** or moves money |
| File organisation inside the granted workspace | Anything that **deletes** (Cowork asks by design — the answer is still "review first") |
| Scheduled tasks producing dated output files | Anything **customer-facing** leaving the building |

**Default rule: draft, never send.** All outbound communication is produced as a draft for human approval. No exceptions for the first 90 days.

## 4. Review habit

- Output of every autonomous run gets human eyes within one business day.
- Each scheduled task has exactly one named owner: see register below.
- Monthly 15-minute review: prune folder grants, connectors, and scheduled tasks; update the context file.

## 6. Honest assumptions this policy makes

Claude makes mistakes; prompt-injection risk is non-zero (Anthropic says so [in its own safety docs](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/13364135-use-claude-cowork-safely)); the business, not the tool, is responsible for what goes out the door. This policy is how a small team carries that responsibility.
